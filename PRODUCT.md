# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Professionals and knowledge workers who write on LinkedIn regularly. They are mid-career or senior, comfortable with tools like Notion, Figma, and Linear. They open this tool for 30-60 seconds to quickly elevate something they already wrote, then leave. Their primary context is a bright workspace during working hours.

## Product Purpose

A focused single-screen utility: paste casual text, get a polished LinkedIn-ready sentence back. Success looks like the user copying the result immediately and feeling confident posting it.

## Brand Personality

Polished, confident, premium. The tool should feel like it belongs alongside Craft.do, Raycast, and Linear: tools that respect the user's taste. Not an AI gimmick. Not a corporate productivity suite.

## Anti-references

- LinkedIn.com itself: no LinkedIn blue (#0a66c2), no rounded navy badges, nothing that looks like it came from the platform.
- Dark developer tools: no terminal-inspired dark-on-dark, no purple/indigo gradient SaaS aesthetics, no neon accents.
- Generic AI writing tools: no sparkle icons, no "magic" gradient buttons, no chatbot-bubble results.
- Sterile corporate white: no flat white with gray borders and Arial.

## Design Principles

1. **Earned weight**: every visual element either aids the task or disappears. Nothing decorates.
2. **Confidence without noise**: a single committed accent color, used deliberately. No gradients doing the work of good design.
3. **Speed feels premium**: the interface loads visually complete and responds without drama. Transitions are brief and purposeful.
4. **Typography leads**: the quality of the result should feel obvious from how text is set. Scale and weight contrast carry hierarchy.
5. **The output is the product**: the rewritten sentence is the hero, not the branding.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Color is never the sole indicator of state. Adequate contrast ratios for both input and result text.
